# StillMind GTM and Business Plan

## Category and Wedge

StillMind is an in-the-moment state-shift app for emotional triggers and overthinking. It is not positioned as therapy, an AI companion, a meditation content library, or a spiritual ranking system.

The initial wedge is a short closed loop:

`trigger -> state route -> suitable practice -> observer distance -> grounded action -> private learning`

The strongest promise is not “feel better.” It is: **create more choice before acting**.

## Beachhead User

Initial ICP hypothesis:

- Adults 22-45 who are generally functional but get pulled into work or relationship triggers.
- Already familiar with mindfulness, journaling, or self-observation, but dislike generic long sessions.
- Need something in the minute before replying, arguing, proving, buying, or replaying.
- Prefer private, local-first use over a chatbot relationship.

The founder's awakening/spiritual community can be the first distribution channel, but public product language remains observation, distance, choice, and grounded action. This prevents the channel from becoming the category.

## What Is Free and What May Be Paid

### Free launch product

- Immediate Reset flow.
- Four core methods: Inner Cinema, paced breath, logout pause, grounded action.
- Local-only history and basic weekly review.
- Data export and deletion.
- No account required.

### StillMind Plus hypothesis

- Full twelve-method library and longer variants.
- Adaptive local routing, favorites, and custom anchors.
- Unlimited review history and richer private pattern views.
- Optional AI cinema and minimized-data weekly summaries.
- Offline packs and deeper guided programs.

The first App Store release should be free unless StoreKit products, restore purchase, entitlement checks, and review credentials are fully configured. A placeholder paywall is worse than no paywall.

## Pricing Experiments

These are hypotheses, not market facts:

| Offer | Test range | What it tests |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Annual Plus | CNY 198-298 | Whether the product earns recurring value |
| Monthly Plus | CNY 28-48 | Lower-commitment demand and churn |
| 21-day guided program | CNY 49-99 | Whether users prefer an outcome package to a library |
| Facilitated cohort | CNY 299-699 | Founder/community leverage and willingness to pay |

Do not optimize price before proving repeated useful resets.

## Six-Week Validation Loop

### Week 0: internal readiness

- TestFlight build installs cleanly.
- Five complete flows pass offline and online.
- Privacy copy matches behavior.
- No raw trigger text enters analytics.

### Weeks 1-2: 15 guided users

- Recruit from founder community, product/design peers, and mindfulness-adjacent users.
- Observe the first session live when possible.
- Ask what happened before opening the app, what felt wrong, and what they did next.
- Success signal: at least 8/15 complete a reset; at least 5/15 report more choice before acting.

### Weeks 3-4: 50 unguided users

- Share TestFlight or public App Store link with a single use-case message.
- Measure first useful reset, seven-day second useful reset, and worse/stopped rate.
- Success signal: >=40% complete first reset, >=20% complete a second useful reset within seven days, adverse/worse+stopped <=15%.

### Weeks 5-6: willingness to pay

- Show two clearly described Plus offers without charging if StoreKit is not ready.
- Run five payment interviews and one price-choice survey.
- Success signal: >=10% qualified intent or at least 5 genuine purchase attempts among repeat users.

## Channel Order

1. Founder-led community and private groups: high trust, fast qualitative learning.
2. Short-form content around concrete moments: “before you send that reply,” not abstract awakening claims.
3. Practitioner/facilitator referrals with a strict non-clinical boundary.
4. App Store search and editorial assets after retention evidence.
5. Carefully scoped employer wellness pilots only after individual privacy architecture is proven.

No paid acquisition before second-use retention is credible.

## Moat Development

| Stage | Moat | Current thickness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prototype | Inner Cinema metaphor and product taste | Thin but memorable |
| Launch | Native flow, twelve-method routing, offline privacy | Moderate product moat |
| Learning | State-method-outcome feedback without raw text | Potential data/decision moat |
| Retention | Personal pattern map and useful weekly experiments | Switching-cost moat |
| Distribution | Trusted community and facilitator playbooks | Channel moat |
| Scale | Evidence-backed routing and validated programs | Stronger system moat |

The method catalog alone is copyable. The defensible asset is the loop that learns which safe method helps which state, under what constraints, while preserving privacy.

## Decision Rules

- If users understand the metaphor but do not return in seven days, improve cueing and occasion fit before adding methods.
- If first completion is low, reduce questions and reading.
- If worse/stopped clusters around a method, suspend that route and investigate before optimizing retention.
- If spiritual wording increases acquisition but reduces mainstream trust, split campaign language, not product safety boundaries.
- If paid intent is weak but repeat use is strong, test one-off programs before subscription.
- If repeat use is weak, do not build accounts, social feeds, or B2B dashboards.

## Explicit Non-Goals

- No diagnosis, treatment, crisis counseling, or guaranteed outcomes.
- No sale of emotional data and no ad model.
- No consciousness score, identity type, or moral streak.
- No unmoderated community feed in the first product.
- No AI dependency for the core experience.
