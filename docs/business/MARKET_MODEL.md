# StillMind Market and Revenue Model

## Method

This is a bottom-up operating model, not a claimed valuation. Public wellness-market totals are too broad to determine StillMind's opportunity. The useful question is how many repeat users the product can reach, retain, and convert within its specific occasion.

## Core Formula

`ARR = monthly active users x paid conversion x annual revenue per paying user`

All inputs below are explicit assumptions that must be replaced with observed data.

| Scenario | MAU | Paid conversion | Annual revenue / payer | Implied ARR |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Validation | 500 | 2% | CNY 198 | CNY 1,980 |
| Early paid | 1,000 | 3% | CNY 240 | CNY 7,200 |
| Base niche | 10,000 | 4% | CNY 240 | CNY 96,000 |
| Growth | 100,000 | 5% | CNY 240 | CNY 1,200,000 |
| Breakout | 1,000,000 | 6% | CNY 300 | CNY 18,000,000 |

These scenarios deliberately expose how much retention and distribution matter. A polished app with 1,000 sporadic users is not yet a venture business.

## Reachable Early Market

For the first 12 months, define the serviceable market operationally:

- People reachable through founder/community content and referrals.
- Chinese-speaking iOS users comfortable with mindfulness-adjacent tools.
- Users with a repeat trigger occasion at least twice per month.
- Users willing to use a non-clinical self-directed product.

An initial SOM target of 1,000-10,000 MAU is more decision-relevant than a broad global meditation TAM.

## Unit-Economics Guardrails

Before paid acquisition:

- Contribution after Apple commission and AI cost should remain positive.
- Optional AI should have a hard budget and deterministic fallback.
- Annual-plan refund rate should stay below 8% as an initial guardrail.
- Paid month-2 retention should exceed 55% before scaling acquisition.
- Target LTV:CAC >=3 only after enough cohorts exist; do not infer LTV from one month.

## Valuation Expectations

StillMind has no defensible standalone valuation before evidence of repeated use. Reasonable milestone framing:

| Evidence | What investors may see |
| --- | --- |
| Prototype only | Founder/product option value; usually not priced from revenue |
| 1,000+ MAU, weak retention | Interesting experiment, not a business |
| 10,000 MAU, strong second-use retention, early paid conversion | Possible pre-seed narrative |
| CNY 1m+ ARR with healthy cohorts and low safety risk | A financeable wellness software business |
| Multi-market growth plus validated routing advantage | Potential venture-scale case |

Do not use a revenue multiple until recurring revenue, retention, and gross margin are observable.

## Highest-Value Unknowns

1. Is the trigger occasion frequent enough for weekly retention?
2. Does deterministic routing improve completion and self-reported agency over one default exercise?
3. Will users trust local history enough to return?
4. Does the founder's spiritual audience transfer to a mainstream product without category confusion?
5. Do repeat users prefer subscription, one-off programs, or facilitated groups?

These unknowns drive the six-week validation plan and should be resolved before expanding infrastructure.
