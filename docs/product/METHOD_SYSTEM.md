# StillMind Method System

## Purpose

StillMind is a state-shift system. It helps a user notice a temporary inner narrative, create distance from it, choose a small practice, and return to deliberate action. It does not diagnose the user, score spiritual attainment, or determine who the user is.

## Routing Inputs

The router uses explicit, session-local answers rather than inferred personality types.

| Dimension | Values | Product use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Activation | low / medium / high | Chooses between reflection and grounding |
| Dominant mode | looping thought / body tension / impulse to act / numbness / relational hurt / quiet curiosity | Narrows method family |
| Available time | 1 / 3 / 5 / 10 minutes | Selects session variant |
| Eyes and body comfort | comfortable / prefer eyes open / avoid body focus | Applies accessibility and safety alternatives |
| Desired outcome | pause / settle / gain distance / release / choose action / practice awareness | Ranks matching methods |
| Prior result | better / same / worse / stopped | Learns preference without making clinical inferences |

## Safety Gate

Before routing, the app checks for immediate danger, medical emergency, inability to stay safe, or a request for diagnosis/treatment. In these cases it does not continue with a deep inward exercise. It offers a short orienting pause and directs the user to local emergency services, a trusted person, or qualified support. StillMind never claims to manage a crisis.

Every practice includes:

- visible stop and exit controls;
- eyes-open alternatives;
- permission to skip or switch methods;
- no breath holding, forceful breathing, or hyperventilation;
- no requirement to forgive, relive, disclose, or reinterpret an event;
- a brief after-check: better, same, worse, or stopped.

## Practice Families

### 1. Inner Cinema

- Mechanism: third-person self-distancing and narrative defusion.
- Best for: looping thoughts, interpersonal triggers, urge to defend or prove.
- Avoid as first choice: very high activation or when reading generated text would add load.
- Core sequence: name trigger -> three short scenes -> role/audience/witness lenses -> grounded action.
- Durations: 1, 3, and 5 minutes.
- Evidence tier: supported.

### 2. Paced Breath

- Mechanism: voluntary slow, comfortable breathing with a longer or equal exhale.
- Best for: bodily activation and urgency when breathing feels safe.
- Alternative: follow the orb visually without changing the breath.
- Core sequence: notice natural breath -> gentle 4/5 or 4/6 rhythm -> release control -> reorient.
- Durations: 1, 3, and 5 minutes.
- Evidence tier: supported for physiological regulation; product outcome claims remain modest.

### 3. Thought Watching

- Mechanism: cognitive defusion; thoughts are noticed as events rather than instructions.
- Best for: repeated thoughts, catastrophizing, arguments rehearsed internally.
- Core sequence: "a thought is here" -> label form, not truth -> watch arrival/stay/departure -> choose next action.
- Durations: 1, 3, 5, and 10 minutes.
- Evidence tier: supported through ACT and related mechanisms.

### 4. Orienting and Wide Gaze

- Mechanism: deliberate visual orienting and widening attention to the present environment.
- Best for: tunnel attention, screen fixation, mild cognitive overload, eyes-open preference.
- Core sequence: find three neutral objects -> notice edges and distance -> widen peripheral field -> return to one task.
- Durations: 1 and 3 minutes.
- Evidence tier: experimental product interpretation. Do not market as a proven treatment.

### 5. Body Scan

- Mechanism: nonjudgmental interoceptive and somatic noticing.
- Best for: diffuse tension when body attention feels acceptable.
- Alternative: contact points only (feet, chair, hands) or wide-gaze practice.
- Core sequence: contact -> temperature/pressure -> tension without fixing -> whole-body field -> reorient.
- Durations: 3, 5, and 10 minutes.
- Evidence tier: supported, with comfort and adverse-response safeguards.

### 6. Person Shift

- Mechanism: changing self-reference from "I am" to "a person is experiencing" or the user's name.
- Best for: fused self-judgment and identity statements.
- Core sequence: original sentence -> third-person sentence -> observable facts -> chosen response.
- Durations: 1 and 3 minutes.
- Evidence tier: supported by self-distancing and third-person self-talk research.

### 7. Logout Pause

- Mechanism: implementation intention and response delay.
- Best for: urge to reply, post, buy, argue, or make an irreversible decision.
- Core sequence: name impulse -> choose pause window -> remove trigger surface -> set return time -> do one neutral action.
- Durations: 1 or 3 minutes plus a user-chosen delay.
- Evidence tier: supported as a planning pattern; specific StillMind implementation requires validation.

### 8. Release and Forgiveness

- Mechanism: optional release of continued mental rehearsal; forgiveness is not reconciliation or excusing harm.
- Best for: persistent relational resentment when the user explicitly chooses it.
- Exclusions: active danger, coercion, or pressure to reconnect.
- Core sequence: acknowledge impact -> separate safety/boundary from mental replay -> release one demand for this moment -> reaffirm boundary or action.
- Durations: 5 and 10 minutes.
- Evidence tier: supported with significant safety/copy constraints.

### 9. Open Awareness

- Mechanism: open monitoring of changing sensations, thoughts, and sounds without selecting one as the self.
- Best for: stable users seeking practice depth, not acute high activation.
- Core sequence: anchor -> open field -> notice change -> rest as observer -> close and orient.
- Durations: 5 and 10 minutes.
- Evidence tier: established contemplative practice family; progression claims require validation.

### 10. Grounded Action

- Mechanism: restore agency through one small, reversible, concrete action.
- Best for: the close of every session.
- Options: drink water, walk, return to one task, wait before replying, write a factual note, contact a trusted person.
- Core sequence: choose -> specify when/where -> make it small -> optionally set reminder.
- Evidence tier: supported by planning and behavior-change research.

### 11. Trigger Journal and Weekly Review

- Mechanism: private pattern reflection without diagnostic labeling.
- Best for: noticing repeated contexts, methods used, and what helped.
- Stored fields: broad trigger category, activation before/after, chosen method, completion, result, optional user-authored note.
- Raw trigger text stays local by default and is excluded from analytics.
- Evidence tier: product hypothesis informed by self-monitoring research; validate retention and burden.

### 12. Anchors and Reminders

- Mechanism: contextual cues and implementation intentions.
- Best for: moving practice from reactive use to daily availability.
- Examples: after unlocking the phone, before opening chat, after a meeting, before sleep.
- Reminders must be user-initiated, quiet, and easy to disable. No shame, streak loss, or urgency copy.
- Evidence tier: supported behavior-change pattern; notification frequency requires experimentation.

## Deterministic Routing Rules

1. If safety gate is positive, route to external support and optional 30-second orienting only.
2. If activation is high, prefer paced breath, orienting, contact-point body scan, or logout pause.
3. If impulse to act is dominant, offer logout pause first and grounded action second.
4. If looping thought is dominant and activation is not high, offer Inner Cinema, thought watching, or person shift.
5. If body tension is dominant, offer paced breath or body scan with a wide-gaze alternative.
6. If numbness/disconnection is selected, prefer sensory orienting and contact points; avoid abstract open awareness first.
7. If relational hurt is selected, offer Inner Cinema or person shift; release/forgiveness only after explicit opt-in.
8. If quiet curiosity is selected, offer open awareness or longer method variants.
9. Rank methods using user favorites and prior "better" results, but always show why and allow manual choice.
10. End every completed route with a grounded action or explicit choice to take no action yet.

## Progression Without Spiritual Ranking

Progress is described through observable practice capabilities:

- Notice: recognize a trigger and name the current mode.
- Pause: create a small gap before acting.
- Shift: use more than one method and choose intentionally.
- Return: resume a valued or necessary action.
- Observe: hold changing thoughts and sensations with less fusion.
- Integrate: notice recurring patterns and adjust environments or boundaries.

These are practice milestones, not levels of human worth, consciousness, morality, or awakening.

## Recommendation Explainability

Every recommendation must say why in plain language, for example:

> You chose high activation and an urge to reply now. A short eyes-open pause is likely to require less reading than Inner Cinema.

No recommendation may cite an inferred trauma, attachment style, diagnosis, ego type, or consciousness score.
