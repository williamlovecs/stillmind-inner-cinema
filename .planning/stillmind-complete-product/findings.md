- Earlier idea of assigning users labels such as comparison/shame/victim/control ego conflicts with the established safety principle. The complete product should describe temporary reaction patterns rather than identity types.
- The user's business intent is not only a utility app: the product is expected to connect to an existing private community and a deeper practice system. The public product still needs a grounded, non-clinical entry point.

## Competitor Benchmark

- Headspace Ebb owns AI conversation, memory, and recommended content after users describe what is happening.
- Wysa owns anonymous, clinically framed emotional support and employer/health-system distribution.
- How We Feel owns precise emotion check-ins, vocabulary, trends, and pattern reflection.
- Balance owns adaptive daily meditation programs and a broad guided-audio catalog.
- Insight Timer owns free content breadth, teachers, live/community surfaces, and discovery.
- The Tapping Solution owns a single named method with strong instruction, social proof, and repeatable sessions.
- Daylio owns extremely low-friction mood/activity logging and correlation charts.
- No benchmarked product owns the exact sequence: emotional trigger -> immediate state shift -> choose among multiple non-clinical distancing methods -> return to one grounded action.
- StillMind should not compete on chatbot warmth, meditation volume, clinical authority, or generic mood tracking. Its wedge is an in-the-moment "exit the role" protocol with method routing and no identity labels.

## Evidence Map: First Pass

- Self-distancing has direct support from a 2022 meta-analysis of observer-perspective reflection (PubMed 36256910).
- Third-person self-talk has experimental support for emotion regulation with relatively low cognitive-control demand (PubMed 28674404).
- Body scan meditation has a 2022 systematic review and meta-analysis, but the product must avoid turning bodily attention into diagnosis (PubMed 35538557).
- Acceptance and Commitment Therapy mechanisms, including cognitive defusion and psychological flexibility, have systematic-review and meta-analytic support, including internet-delivered formats (PubMed 38615492; 36040783).
- Open monitoring is an established meditation family described as nonreactive monitoring of moment-to-moment experience; product claims still need to stay modest and non-clinical (PubMed 18329323 and later reviews).
- Forgiveness interventions have meta-analytic evidence, but the practice must be optional, non-coercive, and must not imply that users should excuse harm or resume unsafe relationships (PubMed 27009829).
- Implementation intentions and planning evidence can support the grounded-action close, but the app should offer choices rather than prescribe a single action (PubMed 39823206).
- Paced breathing, gaze/attention exercises, reminders, and digital-practice harms require a stronger evidence search before final method copy is frozen.

## Product Thesis

- Category entry: a practical emotional-trigger reset, not therapy and not a spiritual-identity product.
- Core mechanism: identify the current state, change vantage point, route to the smallest suitable practice, then return agency through one grounded action.
- Deep framework: a progressive collection of attention, distancing, body-awareness, release, and awareness practices that users can explore without being assigned a personality or spiritual level.
- Retention loop: immediate relief/agency -> saved private session -> method preference learning -> weekly pattern reflection -> gradual practice progression.
- Commercial hypothesis: freemium access to the core reset; subscription for full method library, adaptive paths, weekly review, reminders, offline packs, and deeper programs. Community is a later, moderated layer rather than the initial product surface.
